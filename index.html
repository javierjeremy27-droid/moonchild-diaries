liner notes

who is NIKI?

Long before the arena tours, NIKI was a teenager in Jakarta posting acoustic covers to YouTube, guitar in her lap, learning to say things in English she probably couldn't say out loud. She signed to 88rising in her late teens and has spent the years since turning that same unguarded instinct into full records.

What keeps people coming back isn't just the voice — it's the specificity. She writes about panic attacks, one-sided love, family friction, and the particular loneliness of being far from home, without ever softening the edges. It's diary-writing set to melody, which is exactly why it lands.

Zephyr

debut EP · early sketches
The first fully-produced release, after years of bedroom acoustic covers. Rougher and smaller than what came after, but you can already hear the same instinct for saying the uncomfortable thing plainly.

2020

Moonchild

concept album · sci-fi lullaby
Her debut full-length, built around a moon-mythology concept where sound becomes a kind of shelter. NME named it one of the year's best Asian albums, and it's the record this whole page borrows its name from.
+ liner note
2022

Nicole

self-titled · diary entries
Named after her own first name — and it shows. The concept-album distance of Moonchild drops away for something more direct and personal. This is the record home to "Oceans & Engines," and it charted in both the US and UK.
+ liner note
2024

Buzz

folk-rock · identity crisis
Written on the road during what she's called an identity crisis in her mid-twenties. Warmer, twangier, and more unresolved than what came before — named for the feeling of being right on the edge of something.

track spotlight
Oceans & Engines
from Nicole · 2022

This is the one that gets picked, every time. It's a heartbreak song that never raises its voice — the kind of track that sounds like watching a memory fade in slow motion instead of living through it. There's something about how restrained it stays, even at its most devastating, that makes it feel more honest than a song that begs.
